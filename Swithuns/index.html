Mission statement

StSwithuns's

We are a church of worshipping Christians - who seek to live by faith in Jesus Christ, knowing that the power of God's love, working through the Holy Spirit, transforms the lives of individuals and the community. We are committed to prayer, worship, the study of God's word and fellowship - which help us grow and develop our gifts in the service of God. We believe it is our mission to be at the heart of our community, witnessing to the love of Christ in action and working with God to draw others into our Christian family.

History

Rich and important

The Church of St. Swithuns, Headbourne Worthy, is set within a small woodland area next to a small river coming from Hyde Bourne. Built in early 10th-11th century in commemoration St. Swithun, a Bishop of Winchester who died in 862AD, this Saxon church is a building of substantial architectural importance. The church of St Swithun is located on the Pilgrim's way which is a historic route between Winchester Cathedral and the shrine of Thomas Beckett in Canterbury, Kent.

The Saxon masonry to build this church is of limestone quarried at Binstead on the Isle of Wight. The sturdy S.W Tower, topped by a wooden belfry, was built in the 13th Century as was the south wall of the nave and the channel extension. Leading into the 19th Century, the church went under a lot of restoration including part of the chancel to accommodate the new organ. Above the door of the vestry is the 11th century carved in stone which was originally the outer western wall that represents Christ, flanked by Mary and St. John with the hand of god above.

More recently in 1998, a £100,000 was raised to restore and ring the ancient bells, to build a stairway to a newly constructed bell-chamber. The money was also used to create a new gallery across the west end of the nave. Inside the base of the tower a toilet and kitchenette was built. In addition to these, new lighting and heating was added to modernise the church slightly. In 1999, the Helle organ within the church that had been created circa 1850 with its hand painted pipes was repaired and renovated.

Churches of Kings Worthy

A family

The community of Kings Worthy in Winchester, Hampshire has been here ever since the Saxon times and the two churches here have been linked for a long time. St. Marys and St. Swithuns churches are both historically integral to the area of Kings Worthy. They have both been the centre of the community here even after many centuries of renovations and expansions in addition to being modernised with newer technologies at the turn of the millenium.
