Mission statement

We are a church of worshipping Christians - who seek to live by faith in Jesus Christ, knowing that the power of God's love, working through the Holy Spirit, transforms the lives of individuals and the community. We are committed to prayer, worship, the study of God's word and fellowship - which help us grow and develop our gifts in the service of God. We believe it is our mission to be at the heart of our community, witnessing to the love of Christ in action and working with God to draw others into our Christian family.

The story behind
StSwithun

✝

The Church of St. Swithuns, Headbourne Worthy, is set within a small woodland area next to a small river coming from Hyde Bourne. Built in early 10th-11th century in commemoration St. Swithun, a Bishop of Winchester who died in 862AD, this Saxon church is a building of substantial architectural importance. The church of St Swithun is located on the Pilgrim's way which is a historic route between Winchester Cathedral and the shrine of Thomas Beckett in Canterbury, Kent. The Saxon masonry to build this church is of limestone quarried at Binstead on the Isle of Wight. The sturdy S.W Tower, topped by a wooden belfry, was built in the 13th Century as was the south wall of the nave and the channel extension.
